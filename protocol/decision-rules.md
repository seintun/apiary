# Apiary Decision Rules

## Scout count

- Direct answer: simple, low-risk tasks.
- 2 scouts: moderate complexity or meaningful risk.
- 3 scouts: broad/high-stakes planning or adoption decisions.
- More than 3 scouts: requires explicit justification.

## Quorum sensing

If 2 of 3 scouts agree with confidence >= medium, commit with caveats.

If one high-confidence scout has strong evidence and others do not conflict, commit cautiously or ask the user depending on stakes.

If scouts disagree on substantive facts, spawn one targeted resolving scout. Do not spawn another generalist.

If a devil's advocate or reviewer flags a critical risk with concrete evidence, treat it as a weighted veto until resolved.

## Stop signal

If a devil's advocate/reviewer returns high-confidence `abandon` with concrete evidence, the coordinator may stop sibling scouts unless they are nearly complete.

## Anti-overkill rule

If the synthesis step is taking longer than doing the task directly, stop and simplify.
